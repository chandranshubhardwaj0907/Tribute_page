Major Vikram Batra




Early Life

Major Batra was born on September 9, 1974, in Palampur, Himachal Pradesh, India. He was known by the nickname "Sher Shah."

Education

He completed his schooling at D.A.V. Public School in Palampur and later pursued a bachelor's degree in Arts from Government College, Palampur.

Military Career

Vikram Batra was commissioned into the Indian Army in 1997. He was part of the 13 Jammu and Kashmir Rifles.

Kargil War

Major Batra's most significant achievement was during the Kargil War in 1999. He played a pivotal role in the capture of the crucial peak, Point 4875, in the Dras sector.

Famous Words

During the battle, he uttered the famous words, "Yeh Dil Maange More!" after capturing Point 4875, a phrase that became a symbol of his bravery and determination.

Param Vir Chakra

Major Vikram Batra was posthumously awarded the Param Vir Chakra, India's highest military honor, for his extraordinary courage and supreme sacrifice in the line of duty.

Legacy

He is remembered as a national hero, and his story has inspired countless young Indians to join the armed forces and serve their country.

Family

Major Batra's family has been actively involved in preserving his memory and his contributions to the nation.

Martyrdom

Tragically, Major Vikram Batra made the ultimate sacrifice for his country on July 7, 1999, during a rescue mission.

Honors

Numerous institutions, roads, and memorials have been dedicated to Major Vikram Batra to commemorate his bravery and selflessness.
